Certifications

Google Advanced Data Analytics

After spending the last few month’s post graduation self-studying, I decided that it would be beneficial to get some certifications, I spent some time deciding which would have the most impact on furthering my carreer and choose to do the recently launched Google Advanced Data Analytics Certification.

The main difference I could see between the original and newly released certification was that Google had opted to teaching Python as opposed to R, the later of which is mainly used in academic settings as opposed to in the industry, they had also added a machine learning section and a statistics section to the course. Overall, given my background in mathematics, the time I had spent myself studying machine learning, and my experience programming in Python, I don’t feel I gained much when it came to technical skills, however I learned alot about how to structure my own projects using what Google calls the PACE (Plan, Analyze, Construct, Execute) workflow, the course had also helped me in better understanding the steps I need to take through out the EDA process.

If you’re new to the field I’d strongly recommend taking the certification, it may be a little more difficult that the original, but I’d say overall it’s definitely worth it.

DeepLearning.AI Deep Learning Specialization

The Deep Learning Specialization taught by Andrew Ng is definitely something I learned a lot from. The course is filled with theory meant to educate more on how neural networks function, there’s plenty of mathematics but none of it feels overwhelming, Andrew Ng is exceptionally good at teaching these concepts to anyone who has atleast a high school level of knowledge when it comes to mathematics. Each video is taught in a similar fashion to a university lecture, with an empahsis on understanding the underlying theory behind neural networks, the quizzes and programming assessments further reinforce these concepts, especially given that a majority of the programming assessments do not allow you to use prebuilt layers, instead you have to go through the process of using NumPy or Tensorflow’s mathematical operations to build these layers. Overall the course was a great use of my time as I learned a lot throughout the process.

Reading

After finishing the last course, Sequence Models, in the Deep Learning Specialization, I’ve taken an interest in learning more about the field of natural language processing. I’ve recently picked up a book called Deep Learning For Natural Language Processing by Stephan Raaijmakers and have been learning plenty from it, I look forward to what projects I’ll be able to work on next with what I learn.

Aside from that I’ve been working my way through The Kaggle Book: Data analysis and machine learning for competitive data science by Konrad Banachewicz and Luca Massoron, I’d like to improve my skills in Kaggle competitions and the book has been helpful so far, given that I’m only a few chapters in I don’t feel I’ve picked up any technical knowledge, however its taught me the importance of leveraging tools the the discussion board of each competition and other resources when competing.
